\section{Citation Variations}
Single citations appear in ordinary prose as in \cite{knuth}. Authors also join
multiple keys in one command, for example \cite{lamport,mittelbach}, and then
repeat an earlier source \cite{knuth} after additional commentary.

\subsection{Grouped Discussion}
Review notes sometimes cite three sources together \cite{hoare,dijkstra,brooks}
to summarize design, process, and implementation constraints in one sentence.
The final sentence cites a pair again \cite{lamport,mittelbach} to verify that
repeated multi-citations stay stable across runs.
